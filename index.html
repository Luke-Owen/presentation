THE MOVEMENT

JAN 2017

THE SPARK

A college student writes three words on a poster...

Peter McIndoe, a University of Arkansas student, attends the Women's March in Memphis, Tennessee. Seeing counter-protestors, he grabs a poster and writes "BIRDS AREN'T REAL" on a whim. His friend Ally Perkins films him yelling about government drone birds on the street. The video goes viral. A movement is born.

2017-2018

THE CONSPIRACY VAN

McIndoe drops out of college and goes full method actor...

McIndoe drops out of college, moves to Memphis, and commits fully to the character. He travels the country in a "conspiracy van," giving impassioned speeches about bird drones. Local chapters form organically across the US. Graffiti, chalkboard messages, and stadium chants spread the word: birds aren't real.

2019

BILLBOARDS & MERCH

A billboard goes up in Memphis. The slogans go mainstream...

A billboard reading "BIRDS AREN'T REAL" is erected in Memphis. Merchandise launches with iconic slogans: "IF IT FLIES, IT SPIES" and "BIRD WATCHING GOES BOTH WAYS." Bumper stickers appear on cars nationwide. Some media outlets report on the movement as if it's a genuine conspiracy theory — which is kind of the point.

2021

TWITTER HQ & THE REVEAL

Supporters storm Twitter HQ. Then the New York Times calls...

Hundreds of supporters rally outside Twitter's San Francisco headquarters, demanding they change their bird logo. The movement reaches peak visibility. In December 2021, McIndoe breaks character on the front page of the New York Times, revealing Birds Aren't Real as satirical performance art designed to hold a mirror up to misinformation culture.

2022

60 MINUTES & SXSW

The movement hits mainstream media. 500K+ followers...

McIndoe appears on CBS 60 Minutes explaining how Birds Aren't Real fights "lunacy with lunacy." He speaks at SXSW. The movement surpasses 500,000 followers. Academics begin studying it as a case study in post-ironic activism and media literacy. The line between satire and reality has never been blurrier.

2023

THE TED TALK

McIndoe takes the stage at TED...

Peter McIndoe gives a TED Talk about using absurdist humor to fight misinformation. The movement becomes a legitimate case study in media literacy courses at universities. His message: creating "a little safe space to come together within it and laugh at it, rather than be scared by it."

2024

THE BOOK

850K TikTok followers. 350M+ hashtag views. And now, a book...

Macmillan publishes "Birds Aren't Real: The True Story of Mass Avian Murder and the Largest Surveillance Campaign in US History." The movement has 850,000+ TikTok followers and over 350 million hashtag views. What started as three words on a poster has become Gen Z's defining satirical movement.

WHY IT WORKS

FIGHTING LUNACY WITH LUNACY

Gen Z's answer to QAnon, flat earth, and real conspiracy thinking...

Birds Aren't Real works because it uses the exact language, aesthetics, and passion of real conspiracy movements — but about something obviously absurd. It teaches media literacy through participation. If you can recognize why "birds are drones" is ridiculous, you can start questioning why you believe other things without evidence. The conspiracy isn't about birds. It's about how easy it is to believe anything if it's presented with enough conviction.
